\exercise{What is the difference between the radius of convergence and the interval of convergence?}{The radius of convergence is a \sword{value} $R$ such that a power series, centered at $x=c$, converges for all values of $x$ in $(c-R,c+R)$. The interval of convergence is an \sword{interval} on which the power series converges; it may differ from $(c-R,c+R)$ only at the endpoints.}

\exercise{If the radius of convergence of $\ds\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n$ is 5, what is the radius of convergence of $\ds\sum_{n=1}^\infty n\cdot a_nx^{n-1}$?}{5}

\exercise{If the radius of convergence of $\ds\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n$ is 5, what is the radius of convergence of $\ds\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^na_nx^n$?}{5}
