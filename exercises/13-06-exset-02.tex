\exercise{$D$\label{13_06_ex_07} is bounded by the coordinate planes and\\
 $z=2-\frac23x-2y$.\\
Evaluate the triple integral with order $\dd z\dd y\dd x$.\\
\myincludeasythree{
3Droll=0,
3Dortho=0.0049995239824056625,
3Dc2c=0.6666666865348816 0.6666666865348816 0.3333333134651184,
3Dcoo=-9.639361381530762 -8.587801933288574 36.453922271728516,
3Droo=149.9999987284343}{\marginparwidth}{A tetrahedron with vertices at the origin, (3,0,0), (0,1,0), and (0,0,2).  The face opposite the origin is the plane z=2-2x/3-2y.}{figures/fig13_06_ex_07_3D}}{%
	$\dd z\dd y\dd x$: $\ds\int_0^3\int_0^{1-x/3}\int_0^{2-2x/3-2y}\dd z\dd y\dd x$\\
	$\dd z\dd x\dd y$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_0^{3-3y}\int_0^{2-2x/3-2y}\dd z\dd x\dd y$\\
	$\dd y\dd z\dd x$: $\ds\int_0^3\int_0^{2-2x/3}\int_0^{1-x/3-z/2}\dd y\dd z\dd x$\\
	$\dd y\dd x\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^2\int_0^{3-3z/2}\int_0^{1-x/3-z/2}\dd y\dd x\dd z$\\
	$\dd x\dd z\dd y$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_0^{2-2y}\int_0^{3-3y-3z/2}\dd x\dd z\dd y$\\
	$\dd x\dd y\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^2\int_0^{1-z/2}\int_0^{3-3y-3z/2}\dd x\dd y\dd z$\\
	$\ds V = \int_0^3\int_0^{1-x/3}\int_0^{2-2x/3-2y}\dd z\dd y\dd x =1.$}

\exercise{$D$ is bounded by the coordinate planes and by \\
$y=1-x^2$ and $y=1-z^2$.\\
Do not evaluate  any triple integral. Which order is easier to evaluate: $\dd z\dd y\dd x$ or $\dd y\dd z\dd x$? Explain why.\\
\myincludeasythree{
3Droll=0,
3Dortho=0.004999519791454077,
3Dc2c=0.6666666865348816 0.6666666865348816 0.3333333134651184,
3Dcoo=-11.596293449401855 -12.679614067077637 48.551517486572266,
3Droo=149.9999987284343}{\marginparwidth}{A pyramid with curved sides and vertices at the origin, (1,0,0), (0,1,0), (0,0,1), and (1,0,1).  The face opposite the yz plane is y=1-x^2; the face opposite the xy plane is y=1-z^2.}{figures/fig13_06_ex_13_3D}}{%
$\dd z\dd y\dd x$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_{0}^{1-x^2}\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\dd z\dd y\dd x$\\
$\dd z\dd x\dd y$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\dd z\dd x\dd y$\\
$\dd y\dd z\dd x$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_0^{x}\int_{0}^{1-x^2}\dd y\dd z\dd x + \int_{0}^1\int_x^{1}\int_{0}^{1-z^2}\dd y\dd z\dd x$\\
$\dd y\dd x\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_{0}^{z}\int_{0}^{1-z^2}\dd y\dd x\dd z + \int_0^1\int_{z}^{1}\int_{0}^{1-x^2}\dd y\dd x\dd z$\\
$\dd x\dd z\dd y$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\dd x\dd z\dd y$\\
$\dd x\dd y\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_{0}^{1-z^2}\int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-y}}\dd x\dd y\dd z$\\
Answers will vary. Neither order is particularly ``hard.'' The order $\dd z\dd y\dd x$ requires integrating a square root, so powers can be messy; the order $\dd y\dd z\dd x$ requires two triple integrals, but each uses only polynomials.}

\exercise{$D$ is bounded by the coordinate planes and by \\
$z=1-y/3$ and $z=1-x$.\\
Evaluate the triple integral with order $\dd x\dd y\dd z$.\\
\myincludeasythree{
3Droll=0,
3Dortho=0.0049995239824056625,
3Dc2c=0.6666666865348816 0.6666666865348816 0.3333333134651184,
3Dcoo=-9.639361381530762 -8.587801933288574 36.453922271728516,
3Droo=149.9999987284343}{\marginparwidth}{A pyramid with vertices at the origin, (1,0,0), (0,3,0), (1,3,0), and (0,0,1).  The sloped faces are z=1-x and z=1-y/3.}{figures/fig13_06_ex_14_3D}}{%
	$\dd z\dd y\dd x$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_{0}^{3x}\int_{0}^{1-x}\dd z\dd y\dd x +
	\int_{0}^1\int_{3x}^{3}\int_{0}^{1-y/3}\dd z\dd y\dd x$
	$\dd z\dd x\dd y$: $\ds\int_{0}^3\int_{0}^{y/3}\int_{0}^{1-y/3}\dd z\dd y\dd x+
	\int_{0}^3\int_{y/3}^{1}\int_{0}^{1-x}\dd z\dd x\dd y$
	$\dd y\dd z\dd x$: $\ds\int_{0}^1\int_0^{1-x}\int_{0}^{3-3z}\dd y\dd z\dd x $
	$\dd y\dd x\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_{0}^{1-z}\int_{0}^{3-3z}\dd y\dd x\dd z$
	$\dd x\dd z\dd y$: $\ds\int_{0}^3\int_{0}^{1-y/3}\int_{0}^{1-z}\dd x\dd z\dd y$
	$\dd x\dd y\dd z$: $\ds\int_0^1\int_{0}^{3-3z}\int_{0}^{1-z}\dd x\dd y\dd z$
	$\ds V = \int_0^1\int_{0}^{3-3z}\int_{0}^{1-z}\dd x\dd y\dd z = 1$.}
