\exercisesetinstructions{,  a region is space is described. Set up the triple integrals that find the volume of this region using rectangular, cylindrical and spherical coordinates, then comment on which of the three appears easiest to evaluate.}

\exercise{The region enclosed by the unit sphere, $x^2+y^2+z^2=1$.}{Rectangular: $\int_{-1}^{1}\int_{-\sqrt{1-x^2}}^{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\int_{-\sqrt{1-x^2-y^2}}^{\sqrt{1-x^2-y^2}}\dd z\dd y\dd x$

Cylindrical: $\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1\int_{-\sqrt{1-r^2}}^{\sqrt{1-r^2}}r\dd z\dd r\dd \theta$

Spherical: $\int_0^\pi\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1 \rho^2\sin(\varphi)\dd \rho\dd \theta\dd \varphi$

Spherical appears simplest, avoiding the integration of square-roots and using techniques such as Substitution; all bounds are constants.}

\exercise{The region enclosed by the cylinder $x^2+y^2=1$ and planes $z=0$ and $z=1$.}{Rectangular: $\int_{-1}^{1}\int_{-\sqrt{1-x^2}}^{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\int_{0}^{1}\dd z\dd y\dd x$


Cylindrical: $\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1\int_{0}^{1}r\dd z\dd r\dd \theta$

Spherical: $\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^{\sec\varphi} \rho^2\sin(\varphi)\dd \rho\dd \theta\dd \varphi +
\int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^{\csc\varphi} \rho^2\sin(\varphi)\dd \rho\dd \theta\dd \varphi$

Cylindrical appears simplest, avoiding the integration of square-roots and two triple integrals; all bounds are constants.}

\exercise{The region enclosed by the cone $z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}$ and plane $z=1$.}{Rectangular: $\int_{-1}^{1}\int_{-\sqrt{1-x^2}}^{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\int_{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}^{1}\dd z\dd y\dd x$

Cylindrical: $\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1\int_{r}^{1}r\dd z\dd r\dd \theta$

Spherical: $\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^{\sec\varphi} \rho^2\sin(\varphi)\dd \rho\dd \theta\dd \varphi$

Cylindrical appears simplest, avoiding the integration of square-roots that rectangular uses. Spherical is not difficult, though it requires Substitution, an extra step.}

\exercise{The cube enclosed by the planes $x=0$, $x=1$, $y=0$, $y=1$, $z=0$ and $z=1$. (Hint: in spherical, use order of integration $\dd\rho\dd \varphi\dd \theta$.)}{Rectangular: $\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\dd z\dd y\dd x$

Cylindrical: $\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{\sec\theta}\int_{0}^{1}r\dd z\dd r\dd \theta +
 \int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{\csc\theta}\int_{0}^{1}r\dd z\dd r\dd \theta$

Spherical: $\int _0^{\pi/4}\int _0^{\tan ^{-1}(\sec \theta)}\int _0^{\sec \varphi}\rho ^2 \sin\varphi\dd \rho\dd \varphi\dd  \theta +
	\int _0^{\pi/4}\int _{\tan ^{-1}(\sec \theta)}^{\pi/2}\int _0^{\sec\theta\csc\varphi}\rho ^2 \sin\varphi\dd \rho\dd \varphi\dd  \theta +
	\int _{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int _0^{\tan ^{-1}(\csc \theta)}\int _0^{\sec\varphi}\rho ^2 \sin\varphi\dd \rho\dd \varphi\dd  \theta +
	\int _{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int _{\tan ^{-1}(\csc \theta)}^{\pi/2}\int _0^{\csc\theta\csc\varphi}\rho ^2 \sin\varphi\dd \rho\dd \varphi\dd  \theta$.

Rectangular is clearly the simplest.}
