\exercise{\label{contra_green}Let $\textbf{f}(x,y)=\frac{-y}{x^2 + y^2}\,\textbf{i} + \frac{x}{x^2 + y^2}\,\textbf{j}$ for all $(x,y)\ne(0,0)$, and $C:$ $x=\cos t$, $y=\sin t$, $0\le t\le 2\pi$.
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item Show that $\textbf{f} = \nabla F$, for $F(x,y) = \tan^{-1}(y/x)$.
   \item Show that $\ds\oint_{C}\dotp{f}{dr} = 2\pi$. Does this contradict \autoref{cor:lineintsuffpath}? Explain.
  \end{enumerate}}{(b) No. \emph{Hint:} Think of how $F$ is defined.}

You may assume that $F$ would be smooth. (\emph{Hint: Consider the mixed partial derivatives of $F$.})}{Yes. $F(x,y)=axy+bx+cy+d$.}
