{Let $p$, $q$, and $r$ be constants, and define $f(x)=px^2+qx+r$.  Show that the Mean Value Theorem applied to $f$ for the interval $[a,b]$ is always satisfied at the midpoint of the interval.}
{$2pc+q=\fp(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}=\frac{pb^2+qb+r-pa^2-qa-r}{b-a}=\frac{p(b^2-a^2)+q(b-a)}{b-a}=p(b+a)+q$ implies that $c=\frac{a+b}2$.}
